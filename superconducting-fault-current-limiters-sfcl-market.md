<p><p>Superconducting Fault Current Limiters (SFCL) are devices that protect electrical systems from excessive fault currents, enhancing reliability. The market includes several types: Shielded-Core SFCL, which utilizes a superconducting material within a magnetic shield; Saturable-Core SFCL, which employs a saturable inductor to limit currents; Hybrid Resistive SFCL, combining resistive and superconducting elements for enhanced performance; and Purely Resistive SFCL, which uses resistance to dissipate energy during fault conditions. Each type offers unique benefits for specific applications in power systems.</p></p>
