<p><p>The Helicobacter pylori non-invasive testing market encompasses several diagnostic methods: </p><p>1. **Urea Breath Test**: Patients ingest a substance containing urea; if H. pylori is present, it converts it to ammonia and carbon dioxide, which is then detected in exhaled breath. </p><p>2. **Serology Test**: Blood samples are analyzed to detect antibodies against H. pylori, indicating past or current infection.</p><p>3. **Stool Antigen Test**: Stool samples are tested for H. pylori antigens, providing evidence of an active infection.</p><p>4. **Others**: This category may include newer or less common testing methods.</p></p>
