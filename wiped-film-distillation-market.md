<p><p>Wiped film distillation is a technique used to separate components in liquid mixtures, primarily based on volatility differences. In the vertical market, distillation occurs in a vertical setup, allowing gravity to assist in the movement of the liquid film along the heated surfaces, promoting efficient separation of volatile components. Conversely, the horizontal market features a horizontal arrangement, where liquid is wiped along the heated surface sideways, often improving heat transfer and residence time, suitable for specific applications requiring precise control over the distillation process.</p></p>
