<p><p>Nanoparticle drug delivery systems utilize nanoscale carriers to enhance the therapeutic efficacy of drugs. Liposomes are spherical vesicles composed of lipid bilayers that encapsulate drugs, improving their stability, bioavailability, and targeting capabilities. Lipid nanoparticles, on the other hand, consist of solid or liquid lipids that enable efficient drug delivery, particularly for RNA-based therapies. Both systems facilitate controlled release and minimize side effects, making them crucial in modern pharmaceuticals for targeted treatment strategies and improved patient outcomes.</p></p>
